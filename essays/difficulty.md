## The First Spark
My interests in computers began a decade ago. Back in middle school, a visitor to our class demonstrated the basics of Computer Science to our class, including a simple game. I was fascinated by the idea that you could create a game by writing something called computer code. That moment sparked my curiosity.

## Shifting to Security
As I moved into high school, my interests expanded beyond just writing a computer game. Through internet exploration, I discovered the world of Cybersecurity. I became captivated by the idea of not just building software, but understanding how it could be exploited and how to defend it. This shifted my perspective, and leaned more into Cybersecurity and IT.

## Discovering Machine Learning
I also stumbled upon something new, known as Machine Learning. At first, it mostly seemed like a buzzword, but as I dove deeper, it caught my interest. I was amazed that you could make a computer do complex tasks like predicting, playing games, detecting images, and recognizing patterns by training them with data. This was well before the so-called AI explosion that happened in 2022, and seeing that evolution has made me realize how fast the tech has advanced.

## Navigating the Path Forward
Now, as a university student, I am still navigating my career choice. I am figuring out exactly where I fit in this landscape. I do enjoy the technical problem-solving in IT. I like the idea of programming in Game Dev/Software Engineering. I find myself drawn back to security and infrastructure. I also developed an interest in Machine Learning and Deep Learning. I am thinking maybe I should lean toward DevOps, SQA, and MLOps. While the exact path remains unclear, I am confident that my ideal career lies at the intersection of building robust infrastructure, ensuring software quality, and securing the systems that power the future.
